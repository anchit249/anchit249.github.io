I am a third year student at the Indian Institute of Technology (IIT) Guwahati my where I'm majoring in Chemical Science and Technology. Additionally I'm also pursuing a minor in Biotechnology. My academic interests lie in the fields of Computational Chemistry and Organic Syntesis.

From a young age, my curiosity about the world around me has been insatiable, driving me to inquire deeply into the hows and whys of natural phenomena. Science, with its inherent pursuit of understanding, has always captivated my imagination, and chemistry, in particular, has held a special allure. What fascinates me most about chemistry is its ability to reveal subtle changes, whether in color, smell, or composition, offering insights into the fundamental workings of matter. The sight of sophisticated laboratory equipment, the hum of machines, and the meticulous protocols have always filled me with a sense of wonder and possibility. As a child, I spent hours watching videos detailing processes like extracting gold from electronic parts or converting paper into alcohol, marveling at the ingenuity behind such transformations. The dream of having my own laboratory, where I could conduct experiments and explore the boundaries of scientific knowledge, has been a constant source of inspiration. However, it was my exposure to molecular endocrinology and cancer, during my tenure as a Winter Trainee under Dr. Anil Limaye, that instilled in me a deep-seated desire to contribute meaningfully to humanity's collective well-being. It ignited my passion for utilizing computational chemistry in the pursuit of discovering novel drugs and advancing organic synthesis, having witnessed the potential of science to transform lives.

My journey into the realm of organic synthesis commenced as a Summer Intern at the Department of Chemistry, IIT Bombay, under the guidance of Dr. Santosh J. Gharpure. During this tenure of two months, I worked on synthesizing various substituted carbazoles, for the study of their photophysical properties.

Building upon this experience, I joined Dr. Bhisma K. Patel's, labgroup at IIT Guwahati, and engaged myself in a project centered on green synthesis methodologies. We focused on synthesizing various benzaldoximes through the nucleophilic addition of hydroxylamine to substituted benzaldehyde mediated by KCl in water, followed by a substrate scope of 3,5-diphenylisoxazoles.

Currently, I am working as a Research Intern with Dr. Kalishankar Bhattacharyya, Assistant Professor, Indian Institute of Technology Guwahati. We are designing Thermally Activated Delayed Fluorescence (TADF) molecules and evaulating their viabililty for opto-electronic applications by investigating their electronic properties by applying DFT and other wavefunction-based methods.

Newer Approaches fir Viral Vaccine Development

Anchit Prasad, Vedant Phad, Zakia Farhat, Sachin Kumar* [link]

Bioreactor Design Concepts for Viral Vaccine Production covers a range of interdisciplinary chapters from the engineering perspective of bioreactor design to the biotechnological perspectives of vector design for vaccine development. The book covers bioreactor concepts such as static systems, single-use systems, stirred tanks, perfusion, wave and packed-beds. It reviews options for efficient and economical production of human vaccines and discusses basic factors relevant for viral antigen production in mammalian cells, avian cells, and insect cells. This book will be a great resource for those interested in implemented novel bioreactor design or experimental schemes towards intensified or/and enhanced vaccine production.
